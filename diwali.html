diwali

DIWALI,also called the Festival of Lights, is celebrated across India to mark the victory of good over evil. People light lamps, decorate their homes, exchange sweets, and burst fireworks. It is a time of joy, family gatherings, and prayers for prosperity. diwali or Deepavali, is the Hindu Festival of Lights, symbolizing the victory of good over evil and light over darkness. It is a major festival celebrated by Hindus, Jains, and Sikhs. During the five-day celebration, families clean and decorate their homes with oil lamps (diyas) and colorful lights. People exchange gifts, sweets, and wear new clothes, while also worshipping Goddess Lakshmi for wealth and prosperity. The festival is a time for family togetherness, the exchange of gifts and sweets, and a general atmosphere of joy and celebration. Diwali celebrates Lord Rama's return as a symbol of the victory of good over evil. The festival brings joy, happiness, and light in people's lives. It brings families together and strengthens relationships. The festival of lights spreads the message of brotherhood, unity and love.
